Story of my Life

I was born and raised in Lagos, Nigeria. From a young age, I showed a strong aptitude for learning and was always eager to help others understand difficult concepts. I attended Ordained Leaders International School for my primary education, where I honed my academic skills and developed a passion for mathematics. For my secondary education, I attended Future Mirror Secondary School, where I excelled in my studies and became a respected figure among my peers. I was known for my dedication to learning and my willingness to help others, particularly in the area of mathematics. I participated in several academic competitions, including Cowbellpedia and Spelling Rumble, where I demonstrated my mastery of complex concepts and earned recognition for my achievements. Despite my busy schedule, I remained committed to community service and often spent my free time teaching younger students who struggled with mathematics. My passion for helping others eventually led me to become the best mathematics student in my school and to graduate with excellent results in my Senior Secondary Certificate Examination. In addition to my academic pursuits, I also had several hobbies that I enjoyed. I was an accomplished pianist and spent many hours playing mobile and video games. However, my ultimate career goal was to become a full stack web developer. With my natural talent for problem-solving and my passion for technology, I believed that this career path would allow me to make a positive impact on the world. Overall, My story is one of dedication, hard work, and a desire to help others. my academic achievements and community service have set me on a path towards success, and my passion for technology promises to make me a valuable contributor to the field of web development .... University Experience

My time at university was a whirlwind of academic and extracurricular activities. Overall, my academic journey was positive, with some memorable moments and challenges along the way. In terms of my coursework, I had some great classes and Lecturers who really inspired me to learn and grow. However, there were also times when I felt frustrated with certain classes, especially when the lecturer simply read off their slides without providing much additional explanation or context. Despite this, I persevered and studied hard, with some of my best courses being math and physics during my 100 level year. I remember spending long nights in the lecture theater with my friends, working through practice problems and discussing the material until we felt confident in our understanding. But university life wasn't just about academics for me. I also found myself drawn to various extracurricular activities and clubs on campus. Whether it was joining the debate team, volunteering for a community service project, or playing sports with my friends, I always felt like there was something to do and people to connect with. These experiences helped me to develop new skills, make new friends, and find a sense of purpose and belonging in the university community. Of course, there were also challenges that came with juggling both my academic and extracurricular pursuits. There were times when I felt overwhelmed by the sheer amount of work I had to do, or when I struggled to balance my various commitments. But I always tried to stay focused and organized, and I found that reaching out to friends and advisors for support was key to my success. Overall, my university experience was a time of growth, exploration, and discovery. From my academic coursework to my extracurricular activities, I learned so much about myself and the world around me, and I feel grateful for the opportunities that came my way. .... Read More

Dr Isaiah Babatunde Ayinla

Software Engineering, Data Mining

Dr Ayinla is an exceptional and dedicated lecturer who has been instrumental in shaping my academic career. Over the years, I have taken several courses under his tutelage, including CSC103, CSC235, and CSC293. Dr Ayinla is a demanding teacher who is committed to ensuring that his students understand the course material in-depth. Although he

may not delve deeply into the subject matter during lectures, he challenges us to push beyond our limits and to think critically about the subject matter. His real-world practical exercises have been incredibly helpful to me as a person, and I appreciate his commitment to ensuring that we learn the material thoroughly. However, some students in my department find his assignments stressful, and I also find it challenging whenever he gives us difficult projects with heavy marks. Nevertheless, Dr Ayinla's impact on my academic journey has been invaluable.

In short, Dr. Sakpere is an outstanding lecturer who embodies the qualities of a great teacher.
